For my senior capstone project (completed as a junior), I was part of a team that created an internal-facing data dashboard that allows Court Watch Nola, a New Orleans-based non-profit, to efficiently query their data and create customizable visualizations.

Court Watch NOLA (CWN) is a non-profit organization located in New Orleans, Louisiana that is "dedicated to promoting transparency, equity, and justice through tracking, observing, and reporting thousands of criminal court cases a year." CWN sends well-trained observers into court sessions of all three Orleans Parish court systems (Magistrate, Criminal District Court, and Municipal). Description:

During my participation in the 2022 Future Computing Summer Internship with the National Security Agency, I was part of a team conducting research into the use of machine learning for classifying and generating auxillary arrays for the Reverse Ising Problem. We used discriminatory machine learning models to classify candidate auxillary arrays as either feasible (i.e. a representation that leads to an Ising Circuit that is computationally accurate), or infeasible; we used generative machine learning models--trained on feasible auxillary arrays found by other methods--to generate additional feasible arrays; additionally, we used reinforcement machine learning to teach an agent how to discover feasible arrays.

The Results:

At the end of the summer, our research team documented all of our findings in a final report. My main contributions to the project included (but were not limited to) 1) the development of Generative Adverserial Networks (GANs) to generate feasible auxillary arrays and 2) the development of a novel reinforcement learning approach for discovering auxillary arrays (Sections 3.1.1 and 3.3, respectively). Description:

It is commonplace for plaintiff law firms to regularly issue press releases. For one local law firm, I have been hired to build a press release management and optimization software to manage a $1 million dollar budget.

The firm uses several different national wire services to issue press releases involving cases against publicly traded companies. These releases have budgetary constraints that must comply with each wire service's set of rules, including release frequency, quantity, etc. The budget and the service rules add dozens of constraints that make manually finding the optimal schedule impossible. In a business where any press release has the potential to attract a valuable client, maximizing the number of press releases according to these constraints is invaluable.

This scheduling problem can be represented as a Constraint Satisfaction Problem (CSP). As such, I have implemented a constraint model to maximize the Egalitarian Welfare of the firm's active cases' press releases (i.e. an algorithm that, given active cases and deadlines, maximizes the minimum occuring case). Currently, I am in the process of building a supporting Django web application to support the user side and data processing requirements of a practical solution to the problem.
